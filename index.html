How it works

Three passes, all on your machine.

1

Choose your video

Pick an MP4 at a supported resolution. The file is read straight from disk and never leaves the tab.

2

Lift the star

The exact watermark shape is reverse-blended out of every frame, so detail underneath is recovered rather than blurred.

3

Export clean MP4

Frames are re-encoded to H.264 and muxed back with your original audio track, untouched.

Privacy

Your video never leaves this tab.

Processed on-device

Decoding, watermark removal, and encoding all run through WebCodecs in your browser. There is no server in the loop.

Recovered, not smeared

Because the star is a known overlay, the original blend is inverted per pixel. The result keeps texture a blur or fill would destroy.

Audio stays intact

The original AAC track is copied through to the output without re-encoding, so sound quality is identical to the source.

Yours to keep

The cleaned MP4 is built in memory and handed straight to you as a download. Nothing is stored or logged anywhere.

Questions, answered.

Is anything uploaded to a server? +

No. The whole pipeline runs in your browser using WebCodecs. Your video bytes stay on your device for the entire process.

Why only those four resolutions? +

The remover uses a pixel-accurate mask of the star, and that mask is sized for each export resolution. A clip at a different size would not line up, so those dimensions are required.

Will the audio be kept? +

Yes. The original audio track is passed through to the output MP4 without re-encoding. If a clip has no audio, you get a clean video-only MP4.

Which browsers work? +

A recent Chromium browser such as Chrome or Edge. The tool relies on the WebCodecs API, which those browsers support. It will tell you if the API is missing.

What does "removal strength" do? +

Auto estimates how strongly the star was blended and removes exactly that. Full pushes for a complete erase with edge cleanup. Subtle applies a lighter touch when the watermark is faint.
